Learning strategy

Goals

I don’t know exactly where i want to be or exactly what I want to be doing but my goal is simply to enjoy the work and workspace i enter into, to always be learning and to live by the ocean. My ideal career would be dynamic collaborative and in an ethical field I can be passionate about.

Development

I think at the moment most of my strengths are non technical. I know i’m a good listener and i generally facilitate good communication and energy within a group or team. I have a fairly live-and-let-live attitude, i’m also usually flexible, open minded, logical, resilient and passionate.

My main weaknesses are around feeling like a burden. I can take things too personally and not ask for help when I know it’s needed as I never want to disrupt anyone else's time and energy. I’m also a rubbish public speaker :)

Going forward in this study I would like to work towards developing greater collaboration skills, let go of the idea that I am burdening others with my existence/questions and the ability to confidently public speak. Basically my main weaknesses.

Commitments and success

I am fully committed to this course! In terms of workload I am fairly good at prioritising and have been writing my goals and structure for the day/week down at the beginning of each print and doing my best to meet those commitments. I feel it makes me accountable for my own experience and good for reflecting both during each sprint and as each sprint ends.

Working with other people and in teams is exciting for me so I think I will enjoy the collaboorative nature of this programme. Developing the skills to collaborate effectively and ensure the success of each team i’m in will be a priority for me.

I’m lucky enough to have a partner who has completed dev academy and checks in with me everyday after work This gives me the opportunity to ask any questions I have and seek guidance. My facilitator has also made it super clear I can ask questions at any time and this is really important to me. I may not have been too stuck yet but i really appreciate the offer of open communication because i know the hard stuff is quickly approaching!!

For me success at dev academy will be enjoying my time learning something completely new and developing new and existing skills. From facilitators I only expect i’m encouraged to be curious and find my own success within the team and work we complete. Also hoping my questions are answered ‘kindly’ no matter how un-thought out they are :)
